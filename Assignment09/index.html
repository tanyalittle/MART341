How to Take Photos of Your Child

Instructable

What You Will Need

  • Photography Equipment
    • Any camera (seriously, ANY will do!)
    • Light source (flash, window, outdoors, etc)
  • Child
    • Any child will do!
    • Preferably one that is yours!
  • Optional
    • Props
    • Costumes
    • Reflector
    • Backgrounds
    • Friends!

Step 1: Gather Your Materials

  • Make sure the camera you are using has battery life and enough storage space to save the awesome pictures you're about to take.
  • Gather whatever props or backgrounds you want to use.
  • If you are using a reflector, make sure you have a stand or someone to hold it for you.
  • If you are using a flash, make sure you have batteries for it.
  • If you are using a window, make sure it is clean and free of obstructions.

Step 2: Find a Location

This can be anywhere you and your child feel comfortable:

  • Bedroom
  • Kitchen
  • Bathtub
  • Park
  • Backyard
  • Grocery Store
  • Forest
  • Beach
  • Anywhere!

Step 3: Find an Activity

Do you want to just capture your little being their goofy self? Give them something to do then!

Some ideas:

  • Baking cookies
  • Playing with their toys
  • Taking a bath
  • Helping you at the store
  • Playing in the backyard
  • Playing at the park

Step 4: Find a Light Source

Light will make or break your photos!

Some tips on learning to look for the light and what to do when you find it:

  • Spin your subject around and look for the reflection in their eyes, known as catchlights, to see where the light is coming from.
  • Once you find it, face your subject toward it.
  • Try not to use a flash if you don't have to, use a reflector instead.
    • Reflectors are cheap and easy to use.
    • They come in many different colors, but white is the most common.
    • They can be handheld or on a stand.
    • They can be used to reflect light onto your subject or to block light from your subject.
    • If you don't have one, you can use mirrors, a lit sidewalk, a white shirt, etc!

Step 5: Shooting From Different Angles

Some ideas to try out:

  • Shooting from above.
  • Shooting from head on - get down to their level!
  • Shooting from below - stand them on something to make them feel big!.
  • Shooting from the side - don't forget those profiles!.
  • Shooting from behind.
  • Shooting not just the face!
    • Hands
    • Feet
    • Eyelashes
    • Everything else!

Step 6: Capture Those Memories!

Have fun, smile, dance, and capture the laughter! Your little will only be that age once, so capture them in the moment of their daily lives. Those are the memories you will cherish the most when they're older!

Step 7: Share!

Share your captures with your loved ones!
